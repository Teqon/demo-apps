# Ami Ta Prepara Design Guidelines

Use these guidelines for any Ami Ta Prepara branded design, document, website, app, presentation, or social asset.

## Brand Identity

- The brand should feel modern, clear, urgent, practical, and preparedness-focused.
- Use the hexagon or honeycomb motif as a recurring visual identity element.
- Keep layouts structured, readable, and calm, even when the content communicates urgency.
- Avoid overly cheerful, commercial, decorative, or generic stock-style visuals.

## Logo

- Use the primary Ami Ta Prepara logo for official communication whenever possible.
- The primary logo combines:
  - The "AMI TA PREPARA" wordmark in a clean, modern sans-serif style.
  - Yellow hexagon symbolism built from connected honeycomb shapes.
  - Black, white, and yellow as the standard presentation.
- Secondary variants may be used when space or format requires it:
  - Hexagon symbol without the wordmark.
  - Horizontal or vertical lockups.
  - Compact variants for favicons and social profile images.
- Keep clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of two logo hexagons.
- Do not place text, icons, borders, or images inside the logo clear-space area.
- Minimum logo size:
  - Digital: 80 px wide.
  - Print: 25 mm wide.
- Never distort, stretch, recolor, add shadows, add glows, or place the logo on a busy low-contrast background.

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

- Ami Ta Prepara Yellow: `#FFD22A`
  - RGB: `255, 210, 42`
  - CMYK: `0, 7, 83, 0`
  - Pantone: `PANTONE 123 C`
- Ami Ta Prepara Black: `#1A1A1A`
  - RGB: `26, 26, 26`
  - CMYK: `0, 0, 0, 90`
  - Pantone: `Black 6 C`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
  - RGB: `255, 255, 255`
  - CMYK: `0, 0, 0, 0`

### Secondary Colors

- Dark Gray: `#2E2E2E`
  - RGB: `46, 46, 46`
  - Use for backgrounds and panels.
- Light Gray: `#B3B3B3`
  - RGB: `179, 179, 179`
  - Use for icons and supporting elements.

### Color Usage

- Use yellow only for accents, numbers, icons, and key messages.
- Use black and dark gray as the layout foundation.
- Use white for titles and body text on dark backgrounds.
- Keep icons monochrome or subtly gray unless yellow is needed as an accent.

## Typography

- Primary font family: Brandon Grotesque.
  - Use Bold or Extra Bold for major titles.
  - Use SemiBold or Medium for subtitles and important phrases.
  - Use Regular for standard text.
- Secondary font family: Roboto.
  - Use for long paragraphs, disclaimers, captions, and contact information.
- Use uppercase titles when the design needs urgency and fast readability.
- Important words may be emphasized in yellow or bold.
- Align text left. Do not justify body text.
- Use line-height between `1.2` and `1.4` for readability.

## Photography

- Use realistic, contextual photography connected to preparedness, weather, infrastructure, nature, homes, roofs, rain, flooding, or emergency readiness.
- Prefer images that communicate reality, urgency, and preparation.
- Use semi-transparent gray overlays when needed to create visual consistency and improve text readability.
- Avoid imagery that feels too cheerful, staged, commercial, or disconnected from preparedness.

## Iconography And Illustration

- Use flat line icons with consistent stroke widths.
- Use monochrome or duotone icon styles, especially yellow plus gray.
- Place icons within or near hexagon forms when using them as numbering, category markers, or accents.
- Do not rotate, distort, or recolor icons outside the official palette.
- Use the honeycomb pattern as a background identity element when appropriate.

## Layout And Composition

- Follow this structure for branded communication materials when possible:
  - Logo at the top right.
  - Large white title with generous spacing.
  - Content organized in two columns for information-heavy layouts.
  - Yellow numbered hexagons to mark steps or list items.
  - Footer with social media icons and QR code where relevant.
- Use transparent or dark-gray hexagon structures as background patterns.
- Background hexagons may contain subtle preparedness-related pictograms.
- Maintain generous whitespace around text and icons so dense information remains readable.
- Keep contrast high, especially when placing text over photography or dark patterned backgrounds.

## Practical Design Rules

- Designs should be usable, readable, and direct before they are decorative.
- Preserve the black, white, yellow, and gray identity consistently.
- Use the hexagon language intentionally, not as random decoration.
- Keep all visual systems consistent across print, digital, and social formats.
- When in doubt, choose clarity, contrast, and preparedness-focused realism.
